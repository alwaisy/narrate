They had 48 signups and exactly zero revenue. People were interested enough to create an account, but not enough to pull out a credit card. 

The founder behind PostClaw had to make a decision. Keep building new features to entice them, or change how the product was presented. 

They chose the latter. They recorded a raw, unedited 30-second video of themselves using the tool. No script. No polished marketing. Just typing in a chat and showing the posts going live. 

Within 48 hours, they converted their first two paying customers. Three more followed the next week, pushing them to $150 MRR. 

The logic was simple: you can explain a product all day, but explanation leaves room for doubt. When people see it working, something clicks. Demonstration removes the friction that copy alone can't.
