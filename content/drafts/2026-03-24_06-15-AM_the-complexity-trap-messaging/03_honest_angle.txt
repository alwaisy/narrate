B2B to B2C is not a lateral move. It rewires everything. 

One founder spent 3 months building a SaaS product for individuals using the instincts they learned in B2B software. They packed the first version with features, depth, and a high price point. It looked great for a demo to executives. 

The result from individual users wasn't backlash. It was just silence. 

The honest reality they learned? In B2B, a massive feature list is a confidence signal. It reassures a procurement team. But in B2C, you aren't selling to a budget. You are selling against a Netflix subscription. 

More features just meant more friction. What feels like value to the builder feels like overwhelming homework to the individual user who just wants a quick win. The pivot that saved them was cutting it down to one obvious use case.
