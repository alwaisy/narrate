One founder built for B2B. Another built for B2C.

For the exact same type of software.

Both wanted a successful launch.

The B2B launch:
-> Added massive feature lists.
-> Priced it high.
-> Reassured the procurement team.

The B2C launch (PostClaw):
-> Showed a raw 30-second video.
-> Cut the price in half.
-> Delivered a quick win.

The development time was identical.

B2B project:
- Reassures through complexity.
- Sells to a company budget.
- Survives on features.

B2C project:
- Dies from complexity.
- Sells against a Netflix subscription.
- Survives on simplicity.

They were not building different code.

They were dealing with different psychology.

B2B understands: Complexity is a confidence signal.

B2C believes: Complexity is a churn signal.

The real conversion breakdown:

B2B buyer: Pays for depth.
B2C buyer: Pays for relief from frustration.

Here is what they learned:

Some users buy features.
Most users buy time.

The product is the same.
The translation of value is entirely different.

Because what feels like value to a builder feels like homework to a user.
