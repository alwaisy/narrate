I charge nothing to review indie products.

My first advice? Vague connection apps fail.

Delete everything.

Your endless interest tags. 
Your complex matching algorithms. 
Your generic "say hi" prompts.

Delete it all.

Your social product:

Needs a specific context. 
Needs a low-pressure environment. 
It is about getting offline faster.

THE 5-SECOND RULE:

Lonely people decide in 5 seconds.
When someone opens your app, they see 3 things:

- The context (Why are we meeting?)
- The activity (What are we doing?)
- The safety (Who else is here?)

That is it. That is your shot.

Your community app has ONE job: Move people from screen to real life.

That is your core loop. 
That is where most apps die.

Here is the fix I saw an indie founder use:

1) THE ACTIVITY FORMULA THAT CONVERTS:

Stop: "Find friends near you"
Start: Shared Activity + Clear Goal + Low Pressure

Examples: "Find a GymBuddy to lift with on Tuesdays."

2) THE CONNECTION MONEY PRINTER:

Delete your open-ended chats. Install this instead:

Slot 1: "THE EVENT" - A specific time and place. 
Slot 2: "THE GOAL" - Working out. 
Slot 3: "THE SHIELD" - Strict privacy controls.

Make the reason to meet impossible to misinterpret.

3) TRUST HIERARCHY:

Tier 1: Shared specific pain. 
Tier 2: Mutual interests.
Tier 3: Clear privacy settings. 
Tier 4: Verified profiles. 
Never: Forced vague interactions.
