My biggest lesson on indie hacking:

When you spot a problem you personally suffer from, obsess over it.

Pour your energy into it, make it your only focus.

Naval Ravikant said: 
You have to be deeply passionate about what you are building.

He was right.

But here is what he didn't say:

If you build for a random trend instead, you lose interest fast.
Then your updates slow down.
Then your product becomes a ghost town.

One personal pain point is 10x better than a logical market gap.
And 100x better than copying a trend.

Build accordingly.

The best startup ideas aren't found in market reports.
Ignoring them is the real risk.

I just watched a founder whose eyes light up building an app to escape their lonely work-home routine. Keep building what you need.
