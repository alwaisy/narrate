# Content Pillars
### Awais Alwaisy | Problem-First Indie Product Media

---

## Pillar 1: Problem-First Product Stories

**Core idea:** Cover real indie products by leading with the problem they solve. Not the founder's resume. Not the funding round. The problem.

This is your most differentiated pillar. Nobody else in the indie founder media space does problem-first consistently. You do.

### Content formats that work here
- Weekly teardown: "Why does [problem] exist? Here's the solo-built tool tackling it."
- MRR milestone breakdowns with context on what changed
- "How they found the problem" founder interviews (short LinkedIn format)
- Side-by-side: the problem in the wild (Reddit thread, forum post) vs. the product that answered it

### Why this works for your ICP
Marcus, Priya, and Daniel all consume this to understand if their own problem definitions hold up. They read it like a mirror. It also validates solo founders who feel invisible after shipping.

---

## Pillar 2: The Decisions That Actually Mattered

**Core idea:** Go deep on the specific choices solo founders made. Pricing. Tech stack. First customer channel. What they cut. What they kept. Not lessons. Decisions, with real context.

This separates you from "10 things I learned" content. You're covering the fork in the road, not the destination.

### Content formats that work here
- "Why they chose X over Y" breakdowns (Postgres vs. Mongo, free tier vs. paid from day one)
- Pricing decision case studies: what they charged, what changed, what stuck
- "The one thing they changed that moved the needle" posts
- Distribution choices: what they tried, what failed, what finally worked

### Why this works for your ICP
Arjun and Daniel are obsessed with this. They want the internal logic, not the surface story. This pillar also builds your credibility as someone who actually understands technical and business decisions, not just storytelling.

---

## Pillar 3: Building Without a Safety Net

**Core idea:** The emotional and practical reality of solo founder life. No team. No investors. No guarantee. This pillar covers the honest side: motivation collapse, the moment they almost quit, what sustainable solo building actually looks like day to day.

Not therapy content. Not toxic positivity. Just honest coverage of what this path costs and what it's worth.

### Content formats that work here
- "The moment they almost stopped" founder moments
- What $1k MRR actually changes (and what it doesn't)
- Building in public when you have no audience yet
- The gap between Product Hunt day and month three

### Why this works for your ICP
Seo-Yeon and Priya need this more than strategy content. They need proof that where they are right now is survivable. This pillar builds loyalty, not just readership. It's the one that makes people comment "this is exactly where I am" and follow you for months before they say anything.

## How the Three Pillars Work Together

| Pillar | Job in the Funnel | Reader Action |
|---|---|---|
| Pillar 1: Problem-First Stories | Acquires new readers | Discovers you through a product/problem they recognize |
| Pillar 2: Decisions That Mattered | Converts readers into believers | Trusts you because the depth is real |
| Pillar 3: Building Without a Safety Net | Keeps them coming back | Feels seen. Stays subscribed. Shares with others. |

One pillar acquires. One pillar earns trust. One pillar builds the relationship.

---

## The Editorial Spine Behind All Three

Every post, newsletter, and blog article follows the same framework:

```
Problem → Solution → Product → Founder
```

Everyone else leads with the founder. You lead with the problem. That single choice makes your content more useful to the reader and more credible to the founder being covered.
