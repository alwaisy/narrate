## Persona 1: Marcus

**Name:** Marcus Osei

**Position:** Solo SaaS Founder (formerly Senior Backend Engineer)

---

### Professional Background

Marcus spent 6 years at a fintech company in Berlin. Good salary. Respectable title. Zero ownership. He quit 14 months ago to build a B2B invoicing tool for freelancers in emerging markets. He has real engineering depth. His architecture is clean. His product works. But he's sitting at 4 paying customers and has no idea why it's not growing. He codes from a co-working space in Accra now, keeps a Stripe dashboard tab open at all times, and checks MRR before checking email every morning.

---

### Goals and Objectives

- Get to $1k MRR before his savings run out in 5 months
- Understand why his product isn't converting despite decent traffic
- Find one distribution channel that actually works for him
- Get honest, technical coverage that validates what he built is real

---

### How They Heard About Awais

Marcus found a LinkedIn post covering a solo founder who built a niche tool and hit $2.3k MRR with no audience. The story started with the problem, not the founder's background. He read it three times. Followed immediately. Shared it to a Slack group he's part of with 200 indie builders.

---

### Path to Purchase

Marcus is a reader first. He consumes 3-4 pieces of content from Awais before he does anything. Then he submits his own story through a form or DM. He's not buying a product. He wants coverage. He wants his numbers and decisions to be told honestly to an audience that will understand them. The "purchase" here is trust, then story submission, then becoming a regular source and referral for other founders.

---

### Potential Barriers

- Worried his MRR is too low to be worth covering ("who cares about 4 customers")
- Not sure if his story is interesting enough compared to shinier products
- Skeptical of media that feels like a PR play with no real depth
- Has been burned by "let's collaborate" messages that went nowhere

## Persona 2: Priya

**Name:** Priya Nair

**Position:** Full-Stack Developer + First-Time Founder

---

### Professional Background

Priya works full-time at a product agency in Bangalore. 4 years of experience. Strong React and Node skills. She's been building a side project for 8 months: a tool that helps UX researchers organize and tag qualitative interview data. She launched on Product Hunt 6 weeks ago. 47 upvotes. 3 sign-ups. 0 conversions. She still has her job. She's still building. But the motivation is starting to crack.

---

### Goals and Objectives

- Figure out what she did wrong on the Product Hunt launch so she doesn't repeat it
- Find her first 5 paying customers without spending money on ads
- Understand if her product is solving a real enough problem or if she's building for herself
- Find people who have been exactly where she is and made it through

---

### How They Heard About Awais

She was deep in an Indie Hackers thread titled "Product Hunt launch got zero traction. What now?" Someone linked to a LinkedIn post by Awais that broke down why most indie launches fail at the problem definition stage, not the distribution stage. She clicked. Read the whole thing. Then read 6 more posts back-to-back.

---

### Path to Purchase

Priya follows, saves posts, and lurks for about 3 weeks. Then she comments on a post that feels like it was written about her. Awais replies. That single interaction converts her from reader to true believer. From there she shares posts, refers other builder friends, and eventually submits her own story when she has something worth telling.

---

### Potential Barriers

- Imposter syndrome. She thinks her story isn't ready yet ("I'll reach out when I hit some real number")
- Afraid of being publicly wrong about her product direction
- Has a demanding day job and limited time to engage deeply
- Doesn't have a big audience herself so wonders if being featured will even matter

## Persona 3: Daniel

**Name:** Daniel Kowalski

**Position:** Indie Hacker + Former Product Manager

---

### Professional Background

Daniel was a PM at a mid-size SaaS company in Warsaw for 5 years. He understood roadmaps, user research, prioritization. What he didn't understand was code. So he learned. Took 2 years. Now he can build. He launched his first real product 3 months ago: a lightweight CRM for solo consultants. He's at $340 MRR. Growing slowly. He obsessively reads MRR update threads on Indie Hackers and Twitter. He knows more about other founders' numbers than his own unit economics.

---

### Goals and Objectives

- Get honest outside feedback on his positioning (he suspects it's the problem)
- Learn how other founders made the jump from $300 to $1k MRR specifically
- Build a small but real audience around his build so he has a distribution channel for future products
- Find a media voice he can trust that isn't just hype or survivorship bias

---

### How They Heard About Awais

Daniel follows a lot of indie hacker accounts on LinkedIn. Most of them post the same "10 lessons I learned building my SaaS" content. Then Awais's post showed up. It was different. It started with a problem nobody was solving, moved through the product decision, and ended with a real founder who had real numbers. Daniel screenshot it and saved it to his swipe file.

---

### Path to Purchase

Daniel is analytical. He reads 8-10 posts before he trusts a voice. Then he goes back and reads older posts to check if the quality holds. Once he trusts it, he's a loyal, high-engagement reader who comments thoughtfully and refers others. He'll eventually reach out to pitch his own story when he hits a milestone he thinks is worth telling.

---

### Potential Barriers

- High bar for what counts as "credible" content. He'll disengage if anything feels manufactured.
- Slow to trust new voices after being burned by shallow influencer content before
- Worried $340 MRR is too early to be taken seriously
- Protective of his real numbers. May want to share selectively.

## Persona 4: Seo-Yeon

**Name:** Seo-Yeon Park

**Position:** Designer-Turned-Founder

---

### Professional Background

Seo-Yeon spent 7 years as a UI/UX designer at agencies in Seoul and remotely for US startups. She built interfaces for other people's products. She got good at spotting bad UX. 11 months ago she started building a tool that helps early-stage founders run quick usability tests without hiring a research firm. She's been building in public on Twitter with about 800 followers. 9 paying customers. $210 MRR. She's not sure if she should keep going or pivot the positioning.

---

### Goals and Objectives

- Get her story told by someone who understands both the design and the technical side
- Find out if other founders in similar positions pivoted or pushed through
- Grow her audience beyond Twitter without feeling like she's spamming everywhere
- Validate that $210 MRR with strong retention is more meaningful than 0 MRR with 1000 followers

---

### How They Heard About Awais

A founder she follows on Twitter retweeted a LinkedIn post by Awais about a designer who built a solo product and what made her decisions different from engineer-founders. Seo-Yeon clicked, read it, felt seen in a way most content doesn't make her feel, and followed immediately.

---

### Path to Purchase

Seo-Yeon engages fast. She's already building in public so she's comfortable with visibility. She'll comment within the first week, share a post with her Twitter audience, and DM to ask about being featured within 3-4 weeks of following. She's the persona most likely to bring other founders into Awais's audience through her own network.

---

### Potential Barriers

- Worried her product is too niche to resonate with a broader indie audience
- Has had bad experiences with "media" coverage that felt like it was written by someone who didn't understand her product
- Needs to feel like Awais actually read her work before agreeing to anything
- Protective of her positioning because she's still figuring it out

## Persona 5: Arjun

**Name:** Arjun Mehta

**Position:** Curious Builder (Pre-Launch)

---

### Professional Background

Arjun is 26. Software engineer at a growing startup in Toronto. Good job. Decent pay. Already bored. He has a Notion doc with 23 startup ideas. He's launched nothing. He reads Indie Hackers every day. He has strong opinions about which products will work and which won't. He can build. He just hasn't committed to an idea yet because he's terrified of spending 6 months on the wrong one.

---

### Goals and Objectives

- Find a problem that's real enough to be worth building for
- Understand what "validated" actually looks like before writing a line of code
- Find someone who covers the early, messy, pre-traction stage honestly
- Get a realistic picture of what the first 6 months actually look like so he can mentally prepare

---

### How They Heard About Awais

Arjun found Awais through a LinkedIn algorithm recommendation after engaging with a few indie hacker posts. The post that hooked him was a problem-first breakdown of a tool he'd never heard of, where Awais explained why the problem was real before he even introduced the product. Arjun thought "this is the kind of analysis I've been trying to do in my head for two years."

---

### Path to Purchase

Arjun is a long-game reader. He'll follow for months before doing anything. But when he launches, Awais is the first person he thinks of to cover his story. He's also the persona most likely to share content with his employed-developer friend group, bringing in more pre-launch curious builders into the audience.

---

### Potential Barriers

- No product yet means no story to pitch. He's in consumption mode only for now.
- Overthinks everything. Could stay in research mode for another year.
- Feels like he needs permission to start. Content that gives him that nudge will convert him.
- Not sure if he belongs in a space full of people who have already launched something.
